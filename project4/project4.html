Character Design

- faq -

To get started with designing a character, I often like to gather reference images from the internet. Pinterest is a good source of these, but you can really find them anywhere. Combining a bunch of images together to form an image board for the character can be very helpful as well. Having multiple references for design elements you'd like to integrate makes the process a lot smoother.

It can often be hard to come up with a character design. I recommend checking out sites like Pinterest for inspiration, you can scroll through endless ideas and it can help your thoughts start to form.

When designing characters, I like to start by envisioning their role and creating a moodboard of sorts. After that, I go and gather reference images from various sites, one for the pose and others for the design I envision. From there, I draw a featureless character as a base, and then construct my reference sheet.

The most important thing (to me) in leveling up your designs is to study real cultures and incorporate designs from them into your own. In a fictional setting, consider drawing inspiration from a particular era! Having a cohesive design sense between your characters really takes your designs to the next level.

I believe the design process for this project went very well. From the beginning, I had a general idea in mind for the layout, and I believe I executed it perfectly. Picking a topic I am interested in (character design) helped too, as I was able to give some informed tips that I feel are useful to the up-and-coming designer.
